Introduction to IBM Quantum Compute Platforms

IBM Quantum Computing, often referred to as IBM Q, is a part of IBM's commercial approach to revolutionising information technology by unlocking, understanding, and implementing processing resources to solve complex problems like weather modeling, particle physics, medical research, and predicting financial markets, among others.

IBM offers a range of Quantum systems - each designed with specific quantum volume that ranges from 16 to 128 - available publicly or for commercial use.

IBM Qiskit

One of the primary tools IBM offers for Quantum Computing is Qiskit. Qiskit is an open-source framework for Quantum Computing. It provides tools for creating and manipulating quantum programs and running them on prototype quantum devices on IBM Q Experience as well as simulator on a local machine.

IBM Q Experience

The IBM Q Experience is a cloud-based Quantum Computing platform that allows users to connect to IBMâ€™s quantum processors via IBM Cloud. Users can run algorithms and experiments, work with quantum bits (qubits), and explore tutorials and simulations.

IBM Quantum Network

IBM Quantum Network is a community of start-ups, universities, research labs and Fortune 500 companies working with IBM to advance quantum computing. Members of the IBM Q Network have access to IBM Q's commercial quantum computing systems for research purposes.

Working with IBM Quantum Computing

Here's a simple set of steps to start working with these tools:

  1. Sign up on the IBM Quantum Compute portal or IBM Q Experience.
  2. Choose the system you want to run your program on - there are five quantum systems available at present, including a simulator.
  3. Write and run your Quantum program using Qiskit. You can control actual quantum computers over the internet and run calculations on them in real-time.
  4. Interpret results and improve the algorithm.

Quantum computation is an exciting field of research and technology with a huge potential. IBM's Quantum Compute platforms make this exciting technology accessible to everyone from researchers to hobbyists. For those interested in learning more, IBM provides a wealth of learning resources and a supportive community.
